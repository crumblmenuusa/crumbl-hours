What Time Does Crumbl Open?

Most Crumbl Cookies locations open at 8:00 AM Monday through Saturday. That's earlier than most dessert spots — and it's intentional. Crumbl bakes fresh throughout the day, so opening at 8 AM means cookies are coming out of the oven as early as 9–9:30 AM.

A few location-specific exceptions:

  • Some mall locations open later — 9:00 AM or 10:00 AM — based on mall policies
  • Stores in smaller towns may open at 9:00 AM depending on local demand
  • Urban locations in high-traffic areas generally stick to the 8:00 AM open

If you're visiting specifically for a fresh-out-of-the-oven experience, aim for 9:30 AM to 11:30 AM. That window gives you maximum freshness, shortest lines, and full flavor availability for the Crumbl lineup.

What Time Does Crumbl Close?

Crumbl's closing time depends on the day:

Monday through Thursday: 10:00 PM Standard evening close. If you're planning a weeknight visit, get there before 9:30 PM — popular rotating flavors can sell out before closing, especially earlier in the week when the new lineup drops.

Friday and Saturday: 12:00 AM (Midnight) This is what separates Crumbl from most bakeries. Friday and Saturday, Crumbl stays open until midnight — making it one of the few dessert spots you can hit after a late dinner, a movie, or a night out. The extended weekend hours attract a significant crowd from 8 PM onward, so expect lines if you go late on a Friday or Saturday.

Sunday: Closed No exceptions. Every Crumbl location in the US is closed on Sundays.

Why Is Crumbl Closed on Sundays?

This is the question that surprises most first-time customers — and it comes with a real answer.

Crumbl co-founders Jason McGowan and Sawyer Hemsley made the decision to close every Sunday to give employees a guaranteed day off. The reasoning is straightforward: employees who have time to rest and spend with family perform better and stay longer. For a franchise operation with 29,000+ employees across 1,100+ stores, that matters a lot operationally.

Sunday is also when Crumbl reveals the upcoming week's menu. At 8:00 PM every Sunday, the new rotating lineup goes live on Crumbl's social media and in the app — so Sunday becomes menu anticipation day rather than cookie day. The new flavors hit stores starting Monday morning.

If you need cookies on a Sunday, your options are:

  • Buy extras on Saturday and refrigerate overnight (chilled cookies like Pink Sugar and OREO hold well refrigerated for up to 3 days)
  • Order through a third-party service that may have pre-purchased inventory (rare — most resellers are fans, not official channels)
  • Wait until Monday when the new lineup drops

Crumbl Hours by Location Type

Because Crumbl operates as a franchise, where your store is located affects the hours you should expect:

Standalone Drive-Thru / Strip Mall Locations These follow the national schedule most closely — 8 AM open, 10 PM close weekdays, midnight on weekends. Most Crumbl locations fall into this category.

Mall Locations Hours are often tied to mall operating hours. If the mall opens at 10 AM and closes at 9 PM, your Crumbl may not match the national schedule. Check the mall's hours alongside the Crumbl app.

Urban / High-Traffic Locations Cities like New York, LA, Chicago, and Dallas tend to have locations that stay closer to the midnight close even on weekdays, due to higher demand. Some may also open earlier.

College Town Locations Stores near universities often have extended hours, especially on Thursday nights and weekends, to accommodate late-night traffic.

Small Town Locations May open at 9 AM instead of 8 AM and could close at 9 PM instead of 10 PM on weekdays. Always check before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Crumbl Cookies

Knowing the hours is one thing. Knowing when to show up is another. Here's the breakdown:

Best time for freshest cookies: 9:30 AM – 11:30 AM Crumbl starts baking immediately after opening at 8 AM. Fresh trays are ready within 30–90 minutes of open. Visiting between 9:30 and 11:30 AM gives you the best combination of fresh cookies, minimal wait, and full flavor availability for the week.

Best time for shortest lines: Weekday mornings and early afternoons The period from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM on weekdays is the least crowded window. You'll get full flavor selection without the evening rush.

Busiest time: Friday and Saturday evenings, 6:00 PM – 10:00 PM This is peak Crumbl traffic. After-dinner crowds hit hard on Friday and Saturday, especially when a popular new rotating flavor drops early in the week. If you're visiting during this window, order ahead through the app to skip the line.

Avoid: Late evenings on weekdays Popular rotating flavors — especially NCDs and fan-favorite returning cookies — can sell out before closing on weekdays. If there's a specific rotating cookie you want, don't leave your visit for Thursday night. Go earlier in the week.

Monday morning is always exciting: The new weekly menu drops Monday at 6–8 AM local time. If you want the freshest version of new flavors on their first day, Monday morning visits give you first pick.

Crumbl Hours and the Weekly Menu: How They Connect

Here's something most people don't think about when checking hours: the weekly menu timing matters as much as the store hours.

  • Sunday 8:00 PM EST: Crumbl reveals next week's menu on social media and in the app — while all stores are closed
  • Monday 6:00–8:00 AM local time: New rotating flavors go live in-store
  • Monday through Saturday: All 10 flavors (6 classics + 4 rotating) are available during store hours
  • Thursday only: Thin Thursday cookie available — if you want it, plan a Thursday visit specifically
  • Saturday close: Last chance for the current week's rotating flavors — they're gone after midnight

If your main goal is catching a specific rotating flavor before it disappears, Saturday is your last shot. Plan your visit before Saturday midnight — after that, it's gone until Crumbl decides to bring it back.
